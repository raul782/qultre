At Qultre, we believe the modern world is divided by invisible borders: the lack of practical knowledge about how other societies operate at a local level. We set out to change this by building the **Culture Graph**, a relational database connecting countries, cities, and districts.

Our mission is to empower students, mobile professionals, and curious travelers to understand the real pulse of any neighborhood before they even arrive.

## The Culture Graph Concept

Traditionally, travel and relocation information is fragmented or overly generalized. Qultre breaks down data into three relational layers:
- **Countries**: National laws, generic tax rates, visa policies, and identity.
- **Cities**: Transit protocols, healthcare access, and aggregate cost of living indices.
- **Districts**: Neighborhood customs, hyper-local pricing, street-level safety, and native etiquette helpers.

## Our Information Layers

To validate and build this ecosystem sustainably, we operate on a two-layer model:
1. **Static Layer (Always Free)**: Essential utility information like conversion metrics, primary attractions, translation helpers, and transport fares.
2. **Context Layer (Premium)**: Trend graphs, crowdsourced safety updates, offline utility integrations, and neighborhood price prediction indexes.
